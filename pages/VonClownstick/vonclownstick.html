Nothing is Normal Anymore

Following the results of the 2016 American Presidential election, the world has felt twisted and unfamiliar. Reality has been replaced with unreality.

I have found that most news sources, in an attempt to remain detached and impartial, write their headlines with neutral language that does not reflect the expressionistic nightmare that US national politics has become. To fix this problem, I have taken a cue from Jon Stewart, who in 2013 dubbed our President Elect "VonClownstick," prompting a twitter war in which the President Elect simultaneously revealed his fragility and complete lack of a sense of humor.

Waking up every day to headlines about a buffoon with a proportionally silly name will prevent complacency and avoid normalizing the new state of affairs. The VonClownstick web browser extension replaces the name of our President Elect to drive home how nothing is normal anymore. This will not make the news any less dire, but at the very least it now looks as bizarre as it feels.

Chrome Version

Click on the link above to download a file titled "VonClownstick.crx". In your Chrome browser, open a new tab and open "Extensions" under the Window menu. Drag and drop the .crx file into your list of extensions.

Safari Version

Click on the link above to download a folder titled "VonClownstick.zip." Unzip the folder, then open Safari and select "Extensions" that appears in the "Develop" window. (Enable the "Develop" window by checking "Show Develop menu in menu bar" under "Advanced" in Safari's Preferences.) Click on the "+" icon that appears in the lower left corner, select "Add Extension," and select "VonClownstick.safariextension" from your Downloads folder. Click "Install" and follow the instructions to enable the extension. (If you close Safari, you will need to re-install the extension.)
